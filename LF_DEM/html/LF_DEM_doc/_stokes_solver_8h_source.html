47 class StokesSolver{
48  /*
49  This class provides solver for the "Stokes" equation, which is an equation of motion of the type:
50 
51  ResistanceMatrix*Velocity = SomeForces
52 
53  Here, Velocity can be either translational velocity (U) alone (a 3N vector, F/U case),
54  or more generally translational and rotational velocities (U and W) (a 6N vector, FT/UW case).
55  In the latter case it is stored as (U_1, W_1, U_2, W_2, ...., U_N, W_N).
56  Accordingly, Forces are either forces alone (F) or torques and forces ( F and T, stored as (F_1, T_1, ..., F_N, T_N) ).
57  This solver handles the case for which the ResistanceMatrix is made of two-body short range interactions, i.e. is sparse.
58 
59  The descriptions given here are a priori written for the FT/UW case. Adaptation to the F/U case is straightforward.
60 
61  ============================
62  ============================
63  The ResistanceMatrix has the following structure (all blocks are 6x6 if UW/FT version, 3x3 if U/F version):
64 
65  ================== 6N ==================
66  ......... ..........
67  || | 6x6 . . 6x6 . | ||
68  || | dblock . 0 .odblock . | ||
69  || | . . . | ||
70  || |........................... | ||
71  || | . . | ||
72  || | 0 . dblock . | ||
73  6N | . . | 6N
74  ResistanceMatrix = || |.................. | ||
75  || | . . | ||
76  || |odblock . . | ||
77  || | . . | ||
78  || |......... . | ||
79  || | . | ||
80  || | .........| ||
81  || | . | ||
82  || | . dblock | ||
83  || | . | ||
84  .........
85  =================== 6N =================
86 
87  This matrix is symmetric, so we only store its lower part. This implies dblocks themselves are symmetric.
88  odblocks however are antisymmetric.
89 
90  The non-zero 6x6 blocks are of two kinds:
91  - dblocks ("diagonal") contain the contributions to the force/torque acting on particle i
92  coming from particle i's velocity/angular velocity itself: they are the Stokes drag terms ("self"-terms)
93  on the diagonal, plus parts of the interaction terms coming from lubrication or contact dashpots.
94  - odblocks ("off-diagonal") contain the contributions to the force (torque) acting on particle i
95  coming from other particles velocities and angular velocities.
96 
97  ============================
98  ============================
99  Technical details on the storage of matrix elements:
100 
101  Elements are stored differently depending on which library is used.
102 
103  * CHOLMOD storage:
104  With cholmod library, the storage is first done in the "natural" block structures presented above.
105  At the end of filling, the storage is converted to the compressed-column form used by cholmod.
106 
107  Natural form is done as follows
108  - dblocks: Due to symmetries (dblocks are symmetric, and their "B" subblocks are antisymmetric)
109  each dblock has 18 independent elements (6 diagonal terms and 12 off diagonal).
110  There are np such blocks.
111  They are all stored in 'double *dblocks', which is allocated with size 6*np.
112  Labeling in block associated with particle i is the following:
113  (subblocks names according to "Jeffrey" (Brady) ):
114 
115  | 18*i . . . . . |
116 "A11" subblock (FU) | 18*i+1 18*i+6 . . . . |
117  | 18*i+2 18*i+7 18*i+10 . . . |
118  | 18*i+3 . . 18*i+12 . . |
119 "B11" subblock (TU) | 18*i+4 18*i+8 . 18*i+13 18*i+15 . |
120  | 18*i+5 18*i+9 18*i+11 18*i+14 18*i+16 18*i+17 |
121  "C11" subblock (TW)
122 
123  - odblocks: 24 independent elements per block.
124  Organization is much closer to compressed-column form.
125  All the odblocks values are stored columnwise in 6 vectors called odblocks[0-5]
126  The corresponding locations in the ResistanceMatrix are stored in a vector* called odrows
127  and an array called odrows_table.
128 
129  This works as follows:
130  Particle i interacts with particle j (i<j), we have 1 associated odblock in
131  the ResistanceMatrix. Say j appears as the m^th interaction involving i. Then:
132  odbrows[odbrows_table[i]+m] = j, and the elements are accessed via:
133 
134  (only stored elements are shown) "\tilde B21"
135  | odblocks[0][6*t ] . . odblocks[3][6*t ] . . |
136 "A21" | odblocks[0][6*t+1] odblocks[1][4*t ] . odblocks[3][6*t+1] odblocks[4][4*t ] . |
137  | odblocks[0][6*t+2] odblocks[1][4*t+1] odblocks[2][2*t ] odblocks[3][6*t+2] odblocks[4][4*t+1] odblocks[5][2*t ] |
138  | odblocks[0][6*t+3] . . odblocks[3][6*t+3] . . |
139 "B21" | odblocks[0][6*t+4] odblocks[1][4*t+2] . odblocks[3][6*t+4] odblocks[4][4*t+2] . |
140  | odblocks[0][6*t+5] odblocks[1][4*t+3] odblocks[2][2*t+1] odblocks[3][6*t+5] odblocks[4][4*t+3] odblocks[5][2*t+1] |
141  "C21"
142  with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
143 
144  (all elements) "\tilde B21"
145  | odblocks[0][6*t ] odblocks[0][6*t+1] odblocks[0][6*t+2] odblocks[3][6*t ] -odblocks[3][6*t+1] -odblocks[3][6*t+2] |
146 "A21" | odblocks[0][6*t+1] odblocks[1][4*t ] odblocks[1][4*t+1] odblocks[3][6*t+1] odblocks[4][4*t ] -odblocks[4][4*t+1] |
147  | odblocks[0][6*t+2] odblocks[1][4*t+1] odblocks[2][2*t ] odblocks[3][6*t+2] odblocks[4][4*t+1] odblocks[5][2*t ] |
148  | odblocks[0][6*t+3] -odblocks[0][6*t+4] -odblocks[0][6*t+5] odblocks[3][6*t+3] odblocks[3][6*t+4] odblocks[3][6*t+5] |
149 "B21" | odblocks[0][6*t+4] odblocks[1][4*t+2] -odblocks[1][4*t+3] odblocks[3][6*t+4] odblocks[4][4*t+2] odblocks[4][4*t+3] |
150  | odblocks[0][6*t+5] odblocks[1][4*t+3] odblocks[2][2*t+1] odblocks[3][6*t+5] odblocks[4][4*t+3] odblocks[5][2*t+1] |
151  "C21"
152  with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
153 
154 
155  * TRILINOS storage:
156  TO BE DONE. TRILINOS NOT YET ADAPTED FOR FT/UW VERSION.
157 
158  */
