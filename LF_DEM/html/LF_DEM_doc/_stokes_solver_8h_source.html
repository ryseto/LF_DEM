62 class StokesSolver{
63  /*
64  This class provides solver for the "Stokes" equation, which is an equation of motion of the type:
65 
66  ResistanceMatrix*Velocity = SomeForces
67 
68  Here, Velocity can be either translational velocity (U) alone (a 3N vector, F/U case),
69  or more generally translational and rotational velocities (U and W) (a 6N vector, FT/UW case).
70  In the latter case it is stored as (U_1, W_1, U_2, W_2, ...., U_N, W_N).
71  Accordingly, Forces are either forces alone (F) or torques and forces ( F and T, stored as (F_1, T_1, ..., F_N, T_N) ).
72  This solver handles the case for which the ResistanceMatrix is made of two-body short range interactions, i.e. is sparse.
73 
74  The descriptions given here are a priori written for the FT/UW case. Adaptation to the F/U case is straightforward.
75 
76  ============================
77  ============================
78  The ResistanceMatrix has the following structure (all blocks are 6x6 if UW/FT version, 3x3 if U/F version):
79 
80  ================== 6N ==================
81  ......... ..........
82  || | 6x6 . . 6x6 . | ||
83  || | dblock . 0 .odblock . | ||
84  || | . . . | ||
85  || |........................... | ||
86  || | . . | ||
87  || | 0 . dblock . | ||
88  6N | . . | 6N
89  ResistanceMatrix = || |.................. | ||
90  || | . . | ||
91  || |odblock . . | ||
92  || | . . | ||
93  || |......... . | ||
94  || | . | ||
95  || | .........| ||
96  || | . | ||
97  || | . dblock | ||
98  || | . | ||
99  .........
100  =================== 6N =================
101 
102  This matrix is symmetric, so we only store its lower part. This implies dblocks themselves are symmetric.
103  odblocks however are antisymmetric.
104 
105  The non-zero 6x6 blocks are of two kinds:
106  - dblocks ("diagonal") contain the contributions to the force/torque acting on particle i
107  coming from particle i's velocity/angular velocity itself: they are the Stokes drag terms ("self"-terms)
108  on the diagonal, plus parts of the interaction terms coming from lubrication or contact dashpots.
109  - odblocks ("off-diagonal") contain the contributions to the force (torque) acting on particle i
110  coming from other particles velocities and angular velocities.
111 
112  ============================
113  ============================
114  Technical details on the storage of matrix elements:
115 
116  Elements are stored differently depending on which library is used.
117 
118  * CHOLMOD storage:
119  With cholmod library, the storage is first done in the "natural" block structures presented above.
120  At the end of filling, the storage is converted to the compressed-column form used by cholmod.
121 
122  Natural form is done as follows
123  - dblocks: Due to symmetries (dblocks are symmetric, and their "B" subblocks are antisymmetric)
124  each dblock has 18 independent elements (6 diagonal terms and 12 off diagonal).
125  There are np such blocks.
126  They are all stored in 'double *dblocks', which is allocated with size 6*np.
127  Labeling in block associated with particle i is the following:
128  (subblocks names according to "Jeffrey" (Brady) ):
129 
130 
131  | dblocks[i].col0[0] . . . . . |
132 "A11"(FU)| dblocks[i].col0[1] dblocks[i].col1[0] . . . . |
133  | dblocks[i].col0[2] dblocks[i].col1[1] dblocks[i].col2[0] . . . |
134  | 0 . . dblocks[i].col3[0] . . |
135 "B11"(TU)| dblocks[i].col0[3] 0 . dblocks[i].col3[1] dblocks[i].col4[0] . |
136  | dblocks[i].col0[4] dblocks[i].col1[2] 0 dblocks[i].col3[2] dblocks[i].col4[1] odblocks[t].col5[0] |
137  "C11" subblock (TW)
138 
139  - odblocks: 24 independent elements per block.
140  Organization is much closer to compressed-column form.
141  All the odblocks values are stored in a vector of struct ODBlock.
142  A ODBlock contains 6 arrays called col0, col1, etc, each of them for a column in the block.
143  The corresponding locations in the ResistanceMatrix are stored in a vector* called odrows
144  and an array called odrows_table.
145 
146  This works as follows:
147  Particle i interacts with particle j (i<j), we have 1 associated odblock in
148  the ResistanceMatrix. Say j appears as the m^th interaction involving i. Then:
149  odbrows[odbrows_table[i]+m] = j, and the elements are accessed via:
150 
151  (only stored elements are shown) "\tilde B21"
152  | odblocks[t].col0[0] . . 0 . . |
153 "A21" | odblocks[t].col0[1] odblocks[t].col1[0] . odblocks[t].col3[0] 0 . |
154  | odblocks[t].col0[2] odblocks[t].col1[1] odblocks[t].col2[0] odblocks[t].col3[1] odblocks[t].col4[0] 0 |
155  | 0 . . odblocks[t].col3[2] . . |
156 "B21" | odblocks[t].col0[3] 0 . odblocks[t].col3[3] odblocks[t].col4[1] . |
157  | odblocks[t].col0[4] odblocks[t].col1[2] 0 odblocks[t].col3[4] odblocks[t].col4[2] odblocks[t].col5[0] |
158  "C21"
159  with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
160 
161  Thanks to the symmetries of the resistance matrix, we can recover all the elements as:
162  (all elements) "\tilde B21"
163  | odblocks[t].col0[0] odblocks[t].col0[1] odblocks[t].col0[2] 0 -odblocks[t].col3[0] -odblocks[t].col3[1] |
164 "A21" | odblocks[t].col0[1] odblocks[t].col1[0] odblocks[t].col1[1] odblocks[t].col3[0] 0 -odblocks[t].col4[0] |
165  | odblocks[t].col0[2] odblocks[t].col1[1] odblocks[t].col2[0] odblocks[t].col3[1] odblocks[t].col4[0] 0 |
166  | 0 -odblocks[t].col0[3] -odblocks[t].col0[4] odblocks[t].col3[2] odblocks[t].col3[3] odblocks[t].col3[4] |
167 "B21" | odblocks[t].col0[3] 0 -odblocks[t].col1[2] odblocks[t].col3[3] odblocks[t].col4[1] odblocks[t].col4[2] |
168  | odblocks[t].col0[4] odblocks[t].col1[2] 0 odblocks[t].col3[4] odblocks[t].col4[2] odblocks[t].col5[0] |
169  "C21"
170  with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
171  */
