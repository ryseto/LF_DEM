00047 class StokesSolver{
00048         /*
00049           This class provides solver for the "Stokes" equation, which is an equation of motion of the type:
00050           
00051           ResistanceMatrix*Velocity = SomeForces
00052 
00053           Here, Velocity can be either translational velocity (U) alone (a 3N vector, F/U case), 
00054           or more generally translational and rotational velocities (U and W) (a 6N vector, FT/UW case). 
00055           In the latter case it is stored as (U_1, W_1, U_2, W_2, ...., U_N, W_N).
00056           Accordingly, Forces are either forces alone (F) or torques and forces ( F and T, stored as (F_1, T_1,  ..., F_N, T_N) ).
00057           This solver handles the case for which the ResistanceMatrix is made of two-body short range interactions, i.e. is sparse.
00058           
00059           The descriptions given here are a priori written for the FT/UW case. Adaptation to the F/U case is straightforward.
00060 
00061           ============================
00062           ============================
00063           The ResistanceMatrix has the following structure (all blocks are 6x6 if UW/FT version, 3x3 if U/F version):
00064 
00065                             ================== 6N ==================
00066                              .........        ..........
00067                     ||  |  6x6   .        . 6x6    .            | ||
00068                     ||  | dblock .   0    .odblock .            | ||
00069                     ||  |        .        .        .            | ||
00070                     ||  |...........................            | ||
00071                     ||  |        .        .                     | ||
00072                     ||  |     0  . dblock .                     | ||
00073                     6N  |        .        .                     | 6N
00074  ResistanceMatrix = ||  |..................                     | ||
00075                     ||  |        .           .                  | || 
00076                     ||  |odblock .             .                | ||     
00077                     ||  |        .               .              | ||    
00078                     ||  |.........                 .            | ||
00079                                         ||  |                            .          | ||
00080                                         ||  |                                                  .........| ||
00081                     ||  |                              .        | ||
00082                     ||  |                              . dblock | ||
00083                     ||  |                              .        | ||
00084                                                                            .........
00085                             =================== 6N =================
00086 
00087          This matrix is symmetric, so we only store its lower part. This implies dblocks themselves are symmetric. 
00088          odblocks however are antisymmetric.
00089 
00090          The non-zero 6x6 blocks are of two kinds:
00091          - dblocks ("diagonal") contain the contributions to the force/torque acting on particle i 
00092            coming from particle i's velocity/angular velocity itself: they are the Stokes drag terms ("self"-terms) 
00093            on the diagonal, plus parts of the interaction terms coming from lubrication or contact dashpots. 
00094          - odblocks ("off-diagonal") contain the contributions to the force (torque) acting on particle i 
00095            coming from other particles velocities and angular velocities.
00096 
00097           ============================
00098           ============================
00099           Technical details on the storage of matrix elements:
00100           
00101           Elements are stored differently depending on which library is used.
00102 
00103           * CHOLMOD storage:
00104           With cholmod library, the storage is first done in the "natural" block structures presented above.
00105           At the end of filling, the storage is converted to the compressed-column form used by cholmod.
00106          
00107           Natural form is done as follows 
00108           - dblocks: Due to symmetries (dblocks are symmetric, and their "B" subblocks are antisymmetric)
00109                      each dblock has 18 independent elements (6 diagonal terms and 12 off diagonal).
00110                      There are np such blocks.
00111                      They are all stored in 'double *dblocks', which is allocated with size 6*np.
00112                                  Labeling in block associated with particle i is the following:
00113                                  (subblocks names according to "Jeffrey" (Brady) ):
00114 
00115                                  | 18*i      .        .        .         .         .     |
00116 "A11" subblock (FU)      | 18*i+1   18*i+6    .        .         .         .     |
00117                      | 18*i+2   18*i+7   18*i+10   .         .         .     |
00118                                          | 18*i+3    .        .       18*i+12    .         .     |  
00119 "B11" subblock (TU)      | 18*i+4   18*i+8    .       18*i+13   18*i+15    .     |
00120                                      | 18*i+5   18*i+9   18*i+11  18*i+14   18*i+16  18*i+17 |
00121                                                      "C11" subblock (TW)
00122 
00123           - odblocks: 24 independent elements per block.
00124                       Organization is much closer to compressed-column form.
00125                                   All the odblocks values are stored columnwise in 6 vectors called odblocks[0-5]
00126                                   The corresponding locations in the ResistanceMatrix are stored in a vector* called odrows 
00127                   and an array called odrows_table.
00128 
00129                                   This works as follows:
00130                                   Particle i interacts with particle j (i<j), we have 1 associated odblock in
00131                                   the ResistanceMatrix. Say j appears as the m^th interaction involving i. Then: 
00132                                   odbrows[odbrows_table[i]+m] = j, and the elements are accessed via:
00133 
00134                                   (only stored elements are shown)                                "\tilde B21"
00135                 | odblocks[0][6*t  ]    .                   .                 odblocks[3][6*t  ]    .                   .                |
00136 "A21"   | odblocks[0][6*t+1]  odblocks[1][4*t  ]    .                 odblocks[3][6*t+1]  odblocks[4][4*t  ]    .                |
00137                 | odblocks[0][6*t+2]  odblocks[1][4*t+1]  odblocks[2][2*t  ]  odblocks[3][6*t+2]  odblocks[4][4*t+1]  odblocks[5][2*t  ] |
00138                 | odblocks[0][6*t+3]    .                   .                 odblocks[3][6*t+3]    .                   .                |
00139 "B21"   | odblocks[0][6*t+4]  odblocks[1][4*t+2]    .                 odblocks[3][6*t+4]  odblocks[4][4*t+2]    .                | 
00140                 | odblocks[0][6*t+5]  odblocks[1][4*t+3]  odblocks[2][2*t+1]  odblocks[3][6*t+5]  odblocks[4][4*t+3]  odblocks[5][2*t+1] |
00141                                                                                           "C21"
00142           with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
00143           
00144                      (all elements)                                                   "\tilde B21"
00145                 | odblocks[0][6*t  ]  odblocks[0][6*t+1]  odblocks[0][6*t+2]  odblocks[3][6*t  ] -odblocks[3][6*t+1] -odblocks[3][6*t+2] |
00146 "A21"   | odblocks[0][6*t+1]  odblocks[1][4*t  ]  odblocks[1][4*t+1]  odblocks[3][6*t+1]  odblocks[4][4*t  ] -odblocks[4][4*t+1] |
00147                 | odblocks[0][6*t+2]  odblocks[1][4*t+1]  odblocks[2][2*t  ]  odblocks[3][6*t+2]  odblocks[4][4*t+1]  odblocks[5][2*t  ] |
00148                 | odblocks[0][6*t+3] -odblocks[0][6*t+4] -odblocks[0][6*t+5]  odblocks[3][6*t+3]  odblocks[3][6*t+4]  odblocks[3][6*t+5] |
00149 "B21"   | odblocks[0][6*t+4]  odblocks[1][4*t+2] -odblocks[1][4*t+3]  odblocks[3][6*t+4]  odblocks[4][4*t+2]  odblocks[4][4*t+3] | 
00150                 | odblocks[0][6*t+5]  odblocks[1][4*t+3]  odblocks[2][2*t+1]  odblocks[3][6*t+5]  odblocks[4][4*t+3]  odblocks[5][2*t+1] |
00151                                                                                           "C21"
00152           with t=odbrows_table[i]+m
00153 
00154 
00155           * TRILINOS storage:
00156           TO BE DONE. TRILINOS NOT YET ADAPTED FOR FT/UW VERSION.
00157                                   
00158          */
